// What Volunteering Means Here

This isn't just free labour.

We're aware of how "volunteer" can be exploited. Here's exactly what you get and what we commit to in writing.

[record]

Written Contribution Record

We log your hours, tasks, and contributions. When you leave — or when we start paying — there's a clear record of what you put in. We don't let institutional memory disappear.

[first]

First in Line for Paid Roles

Every paid position we open goes to a current volunteer first, before any external posting. This is a written commitment. Volunteers who've been here longest get first consideration.

[access]

Real Access & Responsibility

You'll have actual access to production systems, real customer interactions, and meaningful ownership of your domain. This is not a coffee-fetching internship — it's real work.

[ref]

Reference Letters & LinkedIn

We write a detailed reference letter for any volunteer who completes three months — specific, honest, and tied to real outcomes. Plus a LinkedIn recommendation.

[infra]

Free Infrastructure Credits

All active volunteers receive KES 2,000 in Pylon infrastructure credits per month — a VM for side projects, a test environment, or something to learn on. No restrictions.

[exit]

Clean Exit — Always

If you need to stop, no hard feelings and no awkwardness. Two weeks' notice so we can transition your work. You keep your reference letter and all credits earned to date.

// Who Fits Here

The kind of people who thrive at Pylon.

We don't have a ping-pong table or unlimited PTO. We have a genuine mission and people who care about it.

You'd rather fix the problem than assign blame.

When something breaks, our instinct is to understand it, fix it, and document what happened — not find who's at fault. We're a small team; blame is expensive.

You communicate when things are unclear.

We're async and distributed. If you're blocked, stuck, or unsure, we need to know. Silence is the only thing that actually slows us down.

You care about East Africa's digital future.

Not as a tagline — as a real conviction. You understand why local infrastructure matters and want to be part of building it, even before it pays well.

You're honest about what you know and don't know.

We'd rather hear "I'm not sure, let me find out" than confident guessing. Our customers rely on accurate information. So do we.

You can work with minimal supervision.

We don't have a manager for every role. You'll own your work, set your own schedule within agreed commitments, and be trusted to deliver.

You're okay with "good enough for now."

We're an early-stage company. Some things are held together with duct tape. The goal is to keep improving — not have everything perfect on day one.
