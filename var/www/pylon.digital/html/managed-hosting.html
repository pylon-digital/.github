// Migration

We move you over.
Free. Zero downtime.

Moving hosting is the part everyone dreads. We've done it hundreds of times — from cPanel, Plesk, runcloud, WP Engine, and plain Linux servers. You don't need to do anything technical.

01
Tell us where you're coming from
Share your current hosting details — cPanel login, SFTP credentials, or SSH access. We'll take it from there and ask you for anything else we need.
02
We clone your site to the new server
Files, database, emails — everything copied to your new environment. We verify the site works on a test URL before you change a single DNS record.
03
You review on the test URL
Check everything looks and works as expected. Tell us if anything needs fixing. We don't move DNS until you're satisfied — no pressure.
04
DNS cutover — you choose when
When you're ready, we switch your DNS to point at the new server. The old server stays live during DNS propagation (usually 15–30 minutes) so there's no gap in service.
05
SSL issued and verified
Once DNS points to us, your SSL certificate is issued automatically. We verify HTTPS is working and HTTP redirects are in place. You're live on Pylon.
Migration fee
Free on all plans. No charge for migration from any provider — shared hosting, VPS, cloud, cPanel, Plesk, Runcloud, or a raw Linux box.
Typical timeline
Most migrations complete in 24–48 hours from when you share credentials. Complex sites with large databases or custom configs may take a day longer.
What we can migrate
WordPress, Laravel, Django, Node.js, Joomla, Drupal, Magento, static sites, and custom PHP apps. Database exports, email accounts, and domain DNS config all transferred.
What we need from you
Access to your current server (cPanel / SFTP / SSH), your domain registrar (to update DNS), and 30 minutes of your time to review the test site before cutover.
Downtime during migration
Zero. Your site stays live on the old server until you confirm the new one is working and approve the DNS switch. There's no window where neither server is serving your site.

// FAQ

Managed hosting questions.

Can't find your answer? Email us or check the full FAQ.

What's the difference between managed hosting and a VM?+

With a VM, you get full root access and manage everything yourself — the OS, web server, database, SSL, security, updates, and your application. You own it all.

With managed hosting, we handle the OS and everything below your application. You get access to your site files, database, and control panel — but you don't manage the server directly. Ideal if you're a developer or business owner who wants their site to work reliably without thinking about sysadmin tasks.

Can I access my server directly via SSH?+

On Starter and Business plans, you access your site via cPanel, SFTP, and phpMyAdmin — no direct SSH to the server. On Enterprise plans, we can arrange SSH access to your dedicated environment.

If you need full server SSH access as a first requirement, a self-managed VM is likely a better fit — you'd have root access and full control.

Can I run a custom PHP version?+

Yes on Business and above — the PHP version selector in cPanel lets you choose the runtime your site uses. We support PHP 7.4 through 8.3. On Enterprise, we can configure a version per-site and lock it for stability. Just tell us what you need.

Is my data stored in Kenya?+

Yes. All managed hosting plans run on our hardware in Nairobi's CBD. Your files, database, and email are physically stored in Kenya. We operate under the Kenya Data Protection Act 2019 and do not transfer data outside Kenya without your explicit consent. We can provide written confirmation of data residency for donors or regulators if needed.

What happens if my WordPress site gets hacked?+

We distinguish between server-level compromise and application-level compromise. If the server itself is breached through a vulnerability on our side — that's on us, and we remediate it as an emergency.

If your WordPress site is compromised through a vulnerable plugin, weak admin password, or outdated theme — that's outside the managed infrastructure SLA, but we'll help you clean it up. Customers with the Managed Security Scan add-on get weekly malware scans, which usually catches issues before they become a full compromise.

Can I host a WooCommerce store?+

Yes — WooCommerce works well on Business and above. Starter is fine for very low-volume stores (a few orders a day), but Business gives you better performance headroom, a staging environment to test plugin updates before going live, and 30-day backup retention — which matters a lot for transactional sites.

For high-volume stores (thousands of orders per day), Enterprise with dedicated resources is the right call. Talk to us and we'll size it correctly.

How long do backups take to restore?+

For most sites, we can restore a backup within 1–2 hours of your request. Large sites with significant database content may take longer. We test restores monthly to confirm they work — we don't want to discover a broken backup when you need it most.

If you need a file-level restore (recovering a deleted file rather than a full site restore), that's usually much faster.

Do you manage email hosting too?+

Each managed hosting plan includes a number of email mailboxes on your domain, managed via cPanel. For businesses that need more mailboxes or a standalone email solution separate from their website hosting, our Business Email service is a better fit — it's designed specifically for teams and scales per-user.
