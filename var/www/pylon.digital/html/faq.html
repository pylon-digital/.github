// General

Basic questions about who Pylon is and how we work.

What is Pylon Digital? +

Pylon Digital is an independent infrastructure company based in Nairobi, Kenya. We provide virtual machines, colocation, managed web hosting, business email, and DNS services — all hosted physically in our Nairobi facility.

We started Pylon because we were frustrated with hosting providers that treated East Africa as an afterthought — servers in Frankfurt, billing in USD, support in the wrong timezone. We built what we wanted to use ourselves.

Where are your servers physically located? +

Our hardware is in a carrier-neutral datacenter in Nairobi's CBD. Not "Africa region." Not Johannesburg. Nairobi. This matters for two reasons:

  • Latency: Kenyan users get ~2ms ping versus 80–180ms from a European node.
  • Compliance: Your data never leaves Kenya, which matters for KDPA and donor data-residency requirements.
ping pylon.digital — try it from a Kenyan network and compare to any Frankfurt IP.
Is Pylon a Kenyan-owned company? +

Yes. Pylon Digital Ltd is registered in Kenya and is 100% East African owned and operated. Our team is based in Nairobi. When you pay us, that money stays in Kenya and funds local salaries, local suppliers, and Kenyan infrastructure.

What makes Pylon different from AWS, GCP, or a cheap overseas VPS? +

Three things that matter most for East African businesses:

  • Physical location: Hyperscalers have no Nairobi presence. The nearest AWS region is Johannesburg. We're in Nairobi.
  • Local billing: We invoice in KES and accept M-Pesa. No FX risk, no international card fees.
  • Human support in your timezone: You can call a Nairobi number during EAT business hours and reach an engineer who knows your account.

We're also fully transparent about our finances — we publish our revenue and expenses monthly. No hyperscaler does that.

Where hyperscalers win: global multi-region deployments, massive auto-scaling, managed Kubernetes. We'll tell you honestly if you need that instead of us.

Can I visit your facility before signing up? +

Yes, and we encourage it for colocation customers especially. Email hello@pylon.digital to schedule a site visit. We'll walk you through the facility, show you the racks, cooling, power setup, and network equipment.

For VM customers, we can also do a remote walkthrough over a video call if you'd like to see how we operate before committing.

// Virtual Machines

Everything about our VM plans, specs, and capabilities.

What operating systems can I run on a VM? +

Any Linux distribution. Our standard image library includes:

  • Ubuntu 22.04 / 24.04 LTS
  • Debian 11 / 12
  • Rocky Linux 9
  • Alpine Linux
  • Fedora (latest stable)
  • Arch Linux

You can also bring your own ISO. We don't currently offer Windows VMs, but colocation customers can run Windows on their own hardware.

Do you oversell VM resources? +

No. When you buy 4 vCPUs and 8GB RAM, that capacity is reserved for your VM — it's not shared with other customers or over-provisioned. We size our host servers to guarantee the resources on every plan.

This is one of the core differences between us and budget overseas VPS providers who over-allocate and hope customers don't all use their full allocation at once.

Guaranteed resources. Not "up to" resources.
What storage type do VMs use? +

All VM storage is NVMe SSD. No spinning disks, no SATA SSDs. NVMe gives you significantly faster random I/O — important for databases, high-traffic web apps, and anything doing heavy disk operations.

If you need additional storage beyond your plan's allocation, contact us — we can attach extra NVMe volumes.

Do I get a dedicated IPv4 address? +

Plans from Basic upwards include one dedicated IPv4 address. The Micro plan is IPv6-only by default, which saves you KES 200/month — but you can add IPv4 for KES 250/month.

All plans include a /64 IPv6 allocation. We're dual-stack (IPv4 + IPv6) throughout our network and peer with KIXP, so IPv6 performance is excellent on Kenyan networks.

Can I get root / administrator access? +

Yes — full root access via SSH. You own the OS and can install, configure, and modify anything. We provision with SSH key authentication (password login disabled by default) and hand over credentials securely.

We don't have any monitoring agents or backdoors on your VM beyond what you install yourself.

Can I upgrade or downgrade my VM plan? +

Yes. Upgrades are straightforward — message us and we'll schedule an upgrade window (usually within 24 hours). It typically requires a brief reboot of under 5 minutes, and we always take a snapshot before making changes.

Downgrades are possible but require that your current disk usage fits within the lower plan's storage. If you're unsure, ask us and we'll check for you.

No data loss on upgrades. Snapshots taken before all changes.
What's the bandwidth limit on VMs? +

VMs are connected to our 10Gbps uplink via a shared switch. There are no hard monthly bandwidth caps on standard plans — we operate on a fair-use basis. If your VM consistently uses high bandwidth that impacts other customers, we'll discuss a custom arrangement.

For high-bandwidth use cases (video streaming, large file distribution, etc.) please talk to us upfront — we can often accommodate with a custom plan.

Do you offer VM backups? +

Daily automated snapshots are available as an add-on for KES 150/month per VM. Snapshots are stored off-site and retained for 7 days.

We also take a one-off manual snapshot before any significant change (OS update, plan upgrade, etc.) at no charge.

Note: VMs are self-managed — we recommend enabling backups for any production workload. We are not liable for data loss on unmanaged VMs without the backup add-on.
Can I run Docker, Kubernetes, or other container workloads? +

Yes. Docker, Docker Compose, Podman, K3s, and similar tools all run fine on our VMs. Full kernel access means no limitations on containerisation.

For multi-node Kubernetes clusters you'll need multiple VMs — we can set up a private network between them. We don't offer a managed Kubernetes service (yet), but we can help you configure K3s or RKE2 on your VMs.

// Colocation

Questions about racking your own hardware in our Nairobi facility.

What colocation options do you offer? +

We offer rack space from 1U up to a full 42U rack in our Nairobi CBD facility:

  • 1U: KES 8,500/mo — 1Gbps shared, /30 IPv4 + /64 IPv6
  • 2U: KES 10,500/mo — 1Gbps shared
  • 4U: KES 14,000/mo — 1Gbps shared
  • Half rack (21U): KES 65,000/mo — 10Gbps shared
  • Full rack (42U): Contact us — 10Gbps dedicated

Additional IPv4 addresses are KES 250/address/month. IPv6 /48 allocations are free.

What power is available per rack? +

Each rack position has access to redundant 240V power with N+1 UPS backing. Our facility runs a generator for continuity during KPLC outages — we've had zero unplanned power-related downtime since 2022.

Standard power per rack unit is 250W. Higher-draw hardware (GPU servers, dense compute) should be declared upfront so we can confirm availability. Full-rack customers can discuss dedicated PDUs.

Do you provide remote hands support for colo customers? +

Yes. Remote hands (physical intervention in your rack space) is available:

  • Standard hours (Mon–Sat, 8am–6pm EAT): KES 2,500/hour
  • Out-of-hours / emergency: KES 5,000/hour

Common remote hands tasks include cable management, rebooting hardware, swapping drives, and connecting/disconnecting equipment. Complex tasks (full server builds, OS installs) are quoted separately.

Can I install my own network equipment? +

Yes. You can rack your own switches, routers, and firewalls. We provide an uplink port (1Gbps or 10Gbps depending on your plan) and your IP allocation — what you do behind that port is your business.

If you want to peer with us or set up BGP, let's talk — we can accommodate that for larger colo customers.

What's the process for shipping hardware to your facility? +

For local deliveries, you can drop hardware off in person or use a courier to our Nairobi address. We'll notify you when it arrives and confirm dimensions and weight before racking.

For international shipments, we recommend using a local clearing agent for customs. We can receive the hardware and handle delivery to the rack once it clears customs. Contact us to coordinate.

// Network & Uptime

How our network is built and what our SLA actually means.

What does your 99.9% uptime SLA cover? +

The 99.9% SLA covers network availability and power to your rack or VM host. In practice, 99.9% allows for about 8.7 hours of downtime per year, or ~43 minutes per month.

SLA credits are calculated as follows:

  • 99.0–99.9% uptime: 10% credit on that month's invoice
  • 95.0–99.0% uptime: 25% credit
  • Below 95.0%: 50% credit

Credits are applied automatically to the following month's invoice. You don't need to file a claim.

Not covered by SLA: Outages caused by your own software/configuration, planned maintenance (with 48hrs notice), or force majeure events.
Who are your upstream providers? +

Our primary upstream transit is Liquid Telecom, one of the largest pan-African network operators. We also peer directly at the Kenya Internet Exchange Point (KIXP), which means traffic between Pylon and other KIXP members (Safaricom, Airtel Kenya, KCB, many Kenyan ISPs and CDNs) stays entirely within Kenya — lower latency and more resilient routing.

Our uplink is 10Gbps dual-stack (IPv4 + IPv6).

Where can I check network status? +

Our public status page is at status.pylon.digital. It shows real-time network status, any active incidents, and historical uptime data.

We update the status page proactively during incidents — we don't wait for customer tickets to trigger an update. If something is down, we already know and are working on it.

You can also subscribe to email or WhatsApp alerts from the status page.

Do you have DDoS protection? +

We have basic volumetric DDoS mitigation in place upstream with Liquid Telecom. For most workloads this is sufficient — the East African DDoS threat landscape is different from, say, Europe or the US.

For high-risk workloads (fintech, public APIs, anything that might attract targeted attacks), we recommend layering a CDN or dedicated DDoS scrubbing service in front of your infrastructure. We're happy to advise on options.

Do you support IPv6? +

Yes — fully. We're dual-stack across our entire network. All customers receive a /64 IPv6 allocation as standard, and we can allocate a /48 for customers who need it (colocation, multi-VM setups).

We peer with KIXP over IPv6, so local IPv6 routing in Kenya is fast and reliable. If you want to run IPv6-only infrastructure, our Micro VM plan is designed for exactly this and saves you KES 200/month over IPv4 plans.

// Billing & Payment

How invoicing, payment, and pricing work at Pylon.

What currency do you bill in? +

We bill in Kenyan Shillings (KES) by default. This means no FX exposure — you know exactly what you'll pay every month regardless of what the USD or EUR rate is doing.

For international customers or enterprise contracts, we can also invoice in USD. Just let us know your preference.

How do I pay? Do you accept M-Pesa? +

Yes — M-Pesa is our most popular payment method. We accept:

  • M-Pesa — Paybill or Till (details on your invoice)
  • Bank transfer — KCB, Equity, Co-op, NCBA
  • Card — Visa or Mastercard via Pesapal
  • USD wire transfer — for international customers

M-Pesa payments confirm instantly. Bank transfers typically clear same day for local banks.

When are invoices sent and when are they due? +

Invoices are sent on the 1st of each month and are due by the 7th. If payment hasn't been received by the 7th, we send one reminder before any action is taken.

Your first invoice is prorated — if you start mid-month, you only pay for the remaining days, calculated to the day.

From month two onwards, you're billed for the full calendar month in advance.

Are there hidden fees or usage-based charges? +

No. Your monthly cost is the fixed amount on your plan, plus any add-ons (backups, extra IPs) you've requested. We don't do:

  • Egress / bandwidth fees
  • API call charges
  • Storage I/O fees
  • Support ticket charges
  • "Surprise" invoice items

The number on your quote is the number on your invoice. If anything changes, you'll hear from us 30 days in advance.

Is VAT included in your prices? +

Yes. All published prices include 16% VAT. What you see is what you pay.

We issue ETR receipts for M-Pesa payments automatically. For bank and card payments, formal KRA-compliant tax invoices are available on request — useful if your organisation needs them for expense claims.

Do you offer discounts or free credits? +

Yes, a few:

  • New customers: KES 2,000 free credit on signup
  • Students: An additional KES 2,000 credit with a valid university email
  • Referrals: KES 500 credit for you and KES 500 for the person you refer, when they become a paying customer
  • NGOs and nonprofits: Contact us — we offer custom rates for organisations operating in East Africa
  • Annual billing: Contact us for a discount on annual pre-payment

// Legal & Compliance

Data protection, KDPA, and regulatory questions.

Are you compliant with the Kenya Data Protection Act (KDPA)? +

Yes. We operate in compliance with the Kenya Data Protection Act, 2019. Specifically:

  • All customer data is stored within Kenya on our Nairobi hardware
  • We do not transfer personal data outside Kenya without explicit customer consent
  • We have a documented data processing policy available on request
  • We respond to data subject access requests within the legally required timeframe

If you need a Data Processing Agreement (DPA) for your organisation's compliance requirements, we can provide one — contact us.

Can you support donor data-residency requirements? +

Yes — this is one of the most common reasons NGOs and development organisations choose us. Many international donors (EU, USAID, UK FCDO, etc.) have data-residency requirements specifying that beneficiary data must be stored in-country or within a specific jurisdiction.

With Pylon, your data is physically in Kenya. We can provide written documentation confirming this for donor reporting purposes.

What is your acceptable use policy? +

Our full AUP is available at pylon.digital/legal/aup. The short version — you may not use Pylon infrastructure for:

  • Sending unsolicited email (spam)
  • Hosting illegal content under Kenyan or international law
  • Launching DDoS or other network attacks
  • Cryptocurrency mining without prior written approval
  • Any activity that degrades service for other customers

Legitimate use cases — web hosting, databases, APIs, VPNs, development environments, email servers — are all fine.

Do you comply with legal requests for customer data? +

We comply with valid legal orders from Kenyan courts and authorities. We do not voluntarily share customer data with any government or third party without a valid legal order.

If we receive a legal request that affects your data, we will notify you as soon as we are legally permitted to do so.

Do you have terms of service and a privacy policy? +

Yes. Both are available at:

  • pylon.digital/legal/terms — Terms of Service
  • pylon.digital/legal/privacy — Privacy Policy
  • pylon.digital/legal/aup — Acceptable Use Policy

If you need a custom Data Processing Agreement for your organisation, email us and we'll arrange one.

// Support

How to reach us and what to expect when you do.

What are your support hours? +

Standard support is available Monday to Saturday, 8am–6pm EAT (UTC+3) via email and WhatsApp.

For network-level emergencies (outages, power events affecting your infrastructure), we have an on-call engineer available 24 hours a day, 7 days a week. The emergency contact number is included in your handover document.

Please reserve the 24/7 emergency line for genuine infrastructure emergencies. For billing questions, configuration help, or non-urgent issues, email or WhatsApp during business hours.
How do I reach support? +

Three ways:

  • Email: hello@pylon.digital — best for non-urgent questions, billing, configuration changes
  • WhatsApp: +254 700 000 000 — best for fast back-and-forth and screenshots
  • Emergency NOC: Number provided in your customer handover document

We don't use a ticket system. You email or message us, and a human reads and responds. Response time during business hours is typically under 2 hours.

What happens if my VM goes down in the middle of the night? +

First, check status.pylon.digital. If there's a network-level event affecting multiple customers, our NOC is already aware and working on it — you'll see an incident update on the status page.

If the status page shows all systems normal and your VM is still unreachable, use the emergency contact in your handover document. If it's an issue with the underlying host (not your configuration), we'll investigate and resolve it.

If it's an issue with your software or configuration on the VM itself, that's outside the SLA — but we can offer paid out-of-hours assistance if needed.

Do you manage the software on my VM for me? +

Standard VMs are self-managed — we handle the hardware, power, and network; you manage the OS and everything running on it.

Managed Hosting customers get OS-level support included — we handle updates, monitoring, and configuration.

If you have a self-managed VM but want occasional sysadmin help, we can quote for that separately. Email us with what you need.

// Account & Changes

Cancellations, migrations, and making changes to your service.

How do I cancel my service? +

Email or WhatsApp us with 30 days' notice. We'll confirm your end date, make sure you've exported any data you need, and close the account cleanly.

There are no cancellation fees on month-to-month plans. If you're on an annual contract, refer to your contract terms.

No penalties. No guilt trips. Just 30 days' notice.
Can I migrate to Pylon from another provider? +

Yes. We can assist with migrations from cPanel, Plesk, or any Linux-based hosting environment at no additional charge for managed hosting customers. For self-managed VMs, we'll advise on the migration process.

The typical migration flow: we provision your new VM or hosting environment, you copy/sync your data to it, then update your DNS. We can help at each step. Most migrations are zero-downtime with proper planning.

Can I migrate away from Pylon if I need to? +

Yes, always. We don't lock you in. Your data is accessible to you at all times via SSH, SFTP, or direct database access. You can export everything without our involvement.

If you're migrating to another provider and need help (rsync, database dumps, DNS advice), we're happy to assist. We'd rather help you leave smoothly than leave a bad impression.

What happens if Pylon closes down? +

We commit to a minimum 60-day notice period if we ever need to wind down operations. During that period we would:

  • Notify all customers immediately by email and WhatsApp
  • Provide full assistance migrating your data to another provider
  • Refund any prepaid amounts on a prorated basis
  • Keep infrastructure running for the full 60-day window

We're also designed to avoid this scenario — our low overhead, transparent finances, and month-to-month billing mean we don't need rapid growth to remain viable.

Can I add additional users or team members to my account? +

Yes. We can add additional SSH keys, support contacts, and billing contacts to your account on request. Just email us the details.

We're working on a more formal multi-user account portal — in the meantime, everything is handled via email and we keep a written record of all authorised contacts for your account.

Will you notify me before making changes that affect my service? +

Yes. Our commitment:

  • Planned maintenance: 48 hours notice minimum, usually more
  • Price changes: 30 days notice
  • Policy changes: 30 days notice
  • Major infrastructure changes: 30 days notice

We notify by email to your registered address. Make sure your contact details are up to date so you don't miss anything.
