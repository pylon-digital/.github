Why carrier-neutral matters
A carrier-neutral facility means you're not locked into a single ISP's infrastructure. You can bring your own transit provider, request cross-connects to other carriers, and take advantage of our KIXP peering independently of your uplink contract.
KIXP peering — what it actually means for you
Traffic between your hardware and other KIXP members (Safaricom, Airtel Kenya, KCB, and many others) never leaves Kenya — it routes locally. This reduces latency to under 5ms for Kenyan ISP end-users and improves resilience if international transit degrades.
Zero planned power downtime since 2022
Our N+1 UPS and diesel generator arrangement has maintained continuous power through every KPLC outage since we opened. The generator spins up in under 10 seconds. We test it monthly.
You can come and look before you sign
We encourage site visits — especially for rack customers committing to significant hardware investments. Email us to arrange a walk-through. We'll show you the racks, cooling, network gear, and answer any technical questions on-site.
KDPA data residency
For NGOs, fintechs, and healthcare organisations with data residency requirements — your hardware and data are physically in Kenya. We can provide written confirmation for donor reporting or regulatory compliance documentation.

// Power & Cooling

Infrastructure you can
actually rely on.

Nairobi's power grid has its moments. We've built around it.

[ups]
UPS — N+1 Redundancy
APC Smart-UPS 10kVA units in N+1 configuration. If one UPS fails, the other carries the full load without interruption. Battery runtime provides bridge power while the generator starts.
Switchover time: <10ms
[gen]
Diesel Generator
Permanently connected diesel generator rated for full facility load. Auto-start on mains failure. Fuel checked weekly — minimum 72-hour runtime maintained at all times.
Generator start: <10 seconds from mains loss
[pdu]
Metered PDUs
Each rack position is served by APC metered PDUs with per-outlet current monitoring. We track power draw in real time — you won't be surprised by a capacity issue at 2am.
Per-outlet monitoring: 15-minute granularity
[cool]
Precision Cooling
CRAC units maintain server inlet temperature below 25°C year-round. Hot-aisle containment panels reduce mixing and improve cooling efficiency. Temperature logged continuously.
Target inlet temp: 18–24°C
[env]
Environmental Monitoring
Temperature, humidity, and smoke sensors throughout the facility. Threshold alerts go to NOC staff immediately. Historical logs available to customers on request.
Humidity target: 40–60% RH
[fire]
Clean Agent Fire Suppression
FM-200 clean agent system — suppresses fire without water or foam that would destroy hardware. Fully compliant with Nairobi County fire regulations and tested annually.
System test: annually by licensed contractor

// FAQ

Colocation questions.

Can't find your answer? Email us directly.

Can I visit the facility before signing up?+

Absolutely — we encourage it, especially for rack customers making a significant hardware commitment. Email hello@pylon.digital to arrange a visit. We'll show you the racks, cooling units, network gear, and power infrastructure in person. No hard sell, just an honest look.

What hardware can I install?+

Any standard 19-inch rackmount equipment — servers, switches, routers, storage arrays, firewalls, GPU compute nodes. We don't restrict operating systems or workloads, subject to the acceptable use policy. High-draw equipment (above standard power allowance) should be declared upfront so we can confirm capacity.

We've hosted HPE ProLiant, Dell PowerEdge, Supermicro, custom storage arrays, Cisco networking gear, and custom GPU builds — if it's rack-mountable, it's fine.

How do I ship hardware to your facility?+

For local delivery, courier to our Nairobi address or bring it yourself during business hours. Email us in advance so we're expecting it. We inspect for visible damage on receipt and send you a photo report.

For international shipments, we recommend using a licensed customs clearing agent — we can recommend local agents familiar with IT equipment imports. We receive the hardware once it clears customs and store it securely until installation.

Can I install my own switches and routers?+

Yes. You can rack your own networking equipment and build your own network behind your uplink port. We provide the uplink port (1Gbps or 10Gbps) and your IP allocation — what happens behind that port is your business. If you want to set up your own BGP session or use your own IP space, talk to us for full-rack customers — it's possible.

What happens during a power outage?+

When KPLC mains power fails, the UPS provides immediate bridge power with under 10ms switchover time — your servers won't notice. The generator starts automatically and is fully supplying the facility within 10 seconds. Our fuel stock is maintained at 72+ hours of runtime minimum, checked weekly.

We haven't had a customer-impacting power event since our UPS and generator setup was upgraded in 2022. You can check our historical uptime at status.pylon.digital.

Is my data subject to KDPA requirements?+

Yes. Since your hardware is physically located in Kenya and we operate under Kenyan law, your data is subject to the Kenya Data Protection Act 2019. This is actually an advantage for most Kenyan organisations — data residency in-country satisfies donor requirements and regulatory expectations. We can provide written confirmation of Kenyan data residency for your compliance documentation.

Can I run my own BGP and announce my own IP space?+

For full-rack customers, yes — we can set up a BGP session to announce your own prefixes. This requires a valid AFRINIC assignment or allocation, a registered ASN, and a valid PeeringDB entry. Contact us to discuss — we handle this case-by-case and there's no additional charge for the peering session itself, though your own IP space requires your own AFRINIC relationship.

What's the minimum contract length?+

Month-to-month for all plans. We don't require a minimum term. If you want an annual contract for a fixed rate or to simplify budgeting, we offer a discount — contact us for the current rate.

If you need to leave, 30 days' notice is all we ask. We'll help you coordinate hardware decommissioning and removal.
