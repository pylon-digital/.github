// Our Story

How we got here.

Pylon grew out of frustration — and a conviction that East African businesses deserve infrastructure built for them.

2018

The original frustration

Our founder was running a fintech startup in Nairobi. Hosting bills came in USD, servers were in Frankfurt, and whenever something broke the support team was offline. There had to be a better way.

2019

First rack, first customers

We racked our first two servers in a carrier-neutral facility in Westlands. Within three months we had eight paying customers — mostly developers and small agencies who'd heard about us through word of mouth.

2021

KIXP peering established

We connected directly to the Kenya Internet Exchange Point. Local latency dropped from 20ms to under 3ms for most Kenyan ISPs. This was the moment Pylon became genuinely competitive on performance.

2023

KES billing launched

We made the switch from USD-only to full KES invoicing with M-Pesa payment. Churn dropped by 40% in six months. Turns out people prefer paying in their own currency.

2025

Three racks, growing team

Today we operate three 42U racks, a small engineering team based in Nairobi, and serve customers from Mombasa to Kampala. We're growing intentionally — quality over quantity.

// How We Operate

Principles, not policies.

We don't have a 60-page terms document that absolves us of responsibility. We have a few things we believe in and try to live by.

We don't oversell resources.

When you buy 4 vCPUs, you get 4 vCPUs. We size our VM hosts so that guaranteed resources are actually guaranteed. You will never notice another customer's traffic spike.

We tell you when something goes wrong.

If there's an outage, degradation, or maintenance affecting your service, we update our status page and email affected customers. We don't wait for you to notice and file a ticket.

We give you enough notice to act.

Price changes, hardware moves, policy updates — 30 days' notice minimum. If we're ever unable to continue serving you, we'll help you migrate somewhere else at our cost.

We respect your data.

We access customer data only to provide the service or when legally required. We don't sell usage patterns, don't serve ads, and don't share your information with third parties.

We price to sustain, not to maximise.

Our target margin is ~15%. Enough to reinvest in hardware, maintain quality, and keep the team employed. Not enough to extract from customers for shareholder returns.

We support the local ecosystem.

Student discounts, NGO pricing, free DNS hosting, KIXP participation — we contribute to East Africa's digital infrastructure, not just charge for it.

// Common Concerns

Things people ask before signing up.

We'd rather address your doubts directly than talk around them.

Concern
"What happens if Pylon closes down?"
Our answer
We commit to a minimum 60-day notice if we ever need to wind down operations, plus full assistance migrating your data out at no charge. We also keep operating costs low precisely so we're not dependent on rapid growth to survive.
Concern
"You're smaller than AWS — can you really handle our traffic?"
Our answer
For most Kenyan businesses, yes. Our 10Gbps uplink and KIXP peering handle serious workloads. If you're expecting viral scale-to-thousands-of-servers moments, talk to us first — we'll tell you honestly if we're the right fit or refer you elsewhere.
Concern
"Is local hosting actually faster, or is that just marketing?"
Our answer
Test it. Ping us: ping 196.201.x.x from a Kenyan network. Compare that to a ping to any Frankfurt or London IP. The latency difference is real and measurable — typically 2ms vs 120–180ms.
Concern
"What if I need a feature you don't offer yet?"
Our answer
Tell us. We build for our customers, not a product roadmap decided by committee. Several features we offer today — including M-Pesa billing and IPv6-only pricing — came directly from customer requests. If it makes sense for multiple customers, we'll build it.
Concern
"Your prices look higher than overseas VPS providers."
Our answer
On paper, sometimes. But factor in: no FX exposure, no hidden egress fees, faster local performance, KDPA compliance, human support, and the economic benefit of keeping that money in Kenya. Total cost of ownership is usually comparable or lower.
