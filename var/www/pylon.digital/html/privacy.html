Section 01

Who We Are

Pylon Digital Ltd ("Pylon", "we", "us") is a company registered in Kenya and operating from Nairobi. We provide infrastructure and related services including virtual machines, colocation, managed hosting, business email, VPN, and DNS hosting.

This Privacy Policy explains what personal data we collect, why we collect it, how we use it, who we share it with, and what rights you have over it. It applies to:

  • Visitors to pylon.digital and our other web properties
  • Customers who use any Pylon service
  • Contacts who email us, submit forms, or otherwise communicate with us
  • Job applicants, volunteers, and contractors
  • Third parties whose data we process as part of delivering services to our customers

Data controller: For personal data collected in connection with operating our business and website, Pylon Digital Ltd is the data controller under the Kenya Data Protection Act 2019 (KDPA).

Data processor: For personal data that our customers store on Pylon infrastructure as part of their own services, Pylon acts as a data processor on behalf of the customer (the data controller). In those cases, the customer's privacy policy — not this one — governs the data subjects' rights.

Plain language: This policy covers how we use your data when you deal with us directly. If your data is stored by a Pylon customer (e.g. a website hosted on our platform), that customer is responsible for your data — not us.

Section 04

Our Lawful Basis for Processing

Under the KDPA, we must have a lawful basis for every category of personal data processing. Here are the bases we rely on and when we use each one:

Contract performance
We need your data to provide the services you've paid for. This covers service delivery, billing, and account management.
Example: using your IP address to provision your VM
Legal obligation
We must retain certain records (invoices, tax documents) and respond to valid legal orders from Kenyan authorities.
Example: keeping invoices for KRA audit purposes
Legitimate interests
We process some data where we have a genuine business interest that doesn't override your rights — such as security monitoring, service improvement, and fraud prevention.
Example: reviewing server logs to detect abuse
Consent
We rely on consent for optional marketing communications to existing customers. You can withdraw consent at any time by unsubscribing or emailing us.
Example: monthly newsletter (opt-in, opt-out anytime)

We do not rely on "vital interests" or "public task" bases for any of our processing activities.

Section 05

How We Use Personal Data

We use personal data in the following specific ways. We do not use personal data for automated decision-making that has legal or similarly significant effects on you.

  • Account management: We use your identity and contact data to create and maintain your account, communicate service updates, and handle your requests.
  • Service provisioning: We use technical data (your IP, SSH keys, domain details) to set up and operate your services. This data is used only for that purpose.
  • Billing: We use your identity, contact, and financial data to issue invoices, process payments, and maintain our accounts. We share transaction references with our payment processor (Pesapal for card payments) but not your bank account details.
  • Support communications: When you contact us, we retain the communication to resolve your issue and for future reference if the same issue recurs. We do not use support communications for marketing purposes.
  • Security logging: We maintain server and network logs to detect abuse, investigate incidents, and meet our obligations under the AUP. Logs are accessed only when a specific security or legal reason exists — not routinely browsed.
  • Service improvement: We use anonymised and aggregated data (no individual identifiers) to understand usage patterns and improve our services and documentation. No individual profiles are built for this purpose.
  • Marketing: We send service-related updates and occasional newsletters to customers who have not opted out. We never send marketing to non-customers, never sell your contact details, and never share them with third parties for their marketing purposes.
  • Legal compliance: Where required by Kenyan law, we use your data to fulfil our obligations — such as issuing KRA-compliant invoices, maintaining transaction records, or responding to court orders.
We do not: sell your data, use it for profiling or targeted advertising, process it with third-party analytics tools, or share it with anyone except as described in Section 6.

Section 07

Data Location & International Transfers

All Pylon customer data — including personal data processed in connection with your account and services — is stored on our hardware physically located in Nairobi, Kenya.

  • Primary storage: Nairobi, Kenya — our carrier-neutral facility in Nairobi CBD. No data is replicated to overseas data centres without your explicit consent.
  • No international transfers: We do not transfer personal data outside Kenya, except where: (a) you have given explicit prior written consent; (b) it is required by a valid Kenyan legal order; or (c) it is strictly necessary for service delivery (for example, routing email to a recipient's overseas server — this is unavoidable when the recipient is overseas).
  • Email transit: When you send email through Pylon email hosting to a recipient with an overseas mail server, your email content necessarily passes through international networks. This is inherent to how email works globally and does not constitute a cross-border personal data transfer under KDPA.
  • Payment processors: Card payment data is processed by Pesapal in accordance with their privacy policy. Pesapal may process transaction data in their own systems. We share only the minimum data necessary for payment processing.
  • KDPA compliance: We comply with the transfer provisions of the KDPA. Where a transfer is necessary and lawful, we document the basis and retain records.
Data residency confirmation: For customers with donor or regulatory requirements specifying Kenyan data residency, we provide written confirmation that data is physically stored in Kenya. Email privacy@pylon.digital to request a confirmation letter.

Section 11

Pylon services are not directed at children under the age of 18. We do not knowingly collect personal data from individuals under 18 years of age.

If you are under 18, please do not use our services or provide us with any personal information. If we become aware that we have inadvertently collected personal data from someone under 18, we will delete that data promptly.

Section 12

Security

We implement technical and organisational measures to protect personal data against unauthorised access, disclosure, alteration, and destruction. These include:

  • Encryption in transit: All connections to Pylon services (HTTPS, IMAPS, SMTPS, SSH, WireGuard) are encrypted. We enforce TLS 1.2 minimum on web and mail services.
  • Encryption at rest: Storage volumes on our infrastructure are encrypted. Passwords and API keys are stored as cryptographic hashes (bcrypt or Argon2) — never in plaintext.
  • Access controls: Personal data is accessible only to Pylon staff with a legitimate operational need. Access is logged and reviewed. No standing root access to production systems.
  • Physical security: Our Nairobi facility has badge-controlled access, CCTV, and a visitor log. Hardware is in a locked, access-controlled environment.
  • Vulnerability management: Security patches are applied to operating systems and services on a regular schedule. We follow responsible disclosure principles for security reports.
  • Data breach response: In the event of a personal data breach that poses a risk to individuals, we will notify the ODPC and affected data subjects within the timeframe required by the KDPA. We maintain an incident response plan and will act promptly.
Your responsibilities: You are responsible for the security of your own credentials (passwords, SSH keys, API keys). Use strong, unique passwords and enable two-factor authentication where available. If you believe your account has been compromised, notify us immediately at hello@pylon.digital.
Section 13

Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time to reflect changes in our practices, services, or legal requirements.

Material changes (changes to what data we collect, why we use it, or who we share it with) will be notified to you by email to your registered address at least 30 days before the change takes effect. You may request deletion of your data during this period if you do not accept the updated policy.

Minor changes (corrections, clarifications, or changes that don't affect how your data is used) may be made without advance notice. The "Last updated" date at the top of this page will reflect any update.

We maintain version history for this policy. If you need a previous version, email privacy@pylon.digital.
