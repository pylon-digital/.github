// Abuse Response Process

What happens after you file a report — step by step.

Every report is read by a human. We don't run automated takedowns — we investigate first, then act. Here is exactly what happens from the moment your report arrives.

01
Receipt
Report received and acknowledged
Email to abuse@pylon.digital or submission via the form below triggers an automated acknowledgement with a reference number. A human NOC operator reads the full report — not a keyword filter.
Acknowledgement: within minutes
02
Triage
Severity assessed and assigned
We classify the report by severity (see tier table below) and assign it to the appropriate engineer. Active attacks and CSAM skip immediately to action.
Triage: within 30 minutes for urgent reports
03
Investigation
We verify the reported activity
We cross-reference the source IP against customer records, review traffic logs, and examine evidence provided. For spam and content issues this takes a few hours. For active attacks we move straight to action.
Investigation: 1–4 hours depending on severity
04
Customer Contact
Responsible customer notified (where safe)
In most cases we contact the responsible customer before taking action — they may have been compromised. We give a remediation window proportional to severity. For CSAM and active attacks there is no notice period.
Notice window: 1 hour (critical) to 24 hours (medium)
05
Action
Remediation enforced
If the customer resolves the issue, we close the report and confirm with you. If not, or if the violation is severe, we null-route the IP, suspend the service, or terminate the account. Account data may be preserved for law enforcement.
Resolution: within 48 hours for most reports
06
Closure
You're notified of the outcome
We email you to confirm the report is resolved. We can't always share customer details, but we'll confirm whether action was taken. Reports closed without action will include an explanation.
Closure notification: same day as resolution

// Legal & Law Enforcement

Formal legal requests, court orders, and government agency enquiries.

Pylon Digital Ltd is a registered Kenyan company operating under Kenyan law. We comply with valid legal orders from Kenyan courts and authorities. We do not voluntarily share customer data with any government, authority, or third party without a valid legal order.

Submitting a legal request: Written requests must be sent to legal@pylon.digital or our registered address. Requests must include the issuing authority, legal basis, scope of information requested, and contact details of the requesting officer. Informal or verbal requests will not be actioned.

Preservation requests: We can preserve records for up to 90 days pending a formal court order, upon written request from a Kenyan law enforcement agency.

Emergency disclosure: In cases involving an imminent, credible threat to life, we may disclose limited information to relevant Kenyan authorities without a court order. Each such disclosure is documented internally and subject to legal review.

Customer notification: We will notify affected customers of legal requests as soon as we are legally permitted to do so, unless prohibited by law, court order, or where notification would compromise an active criminal investigation.

Foreign law enforcement: Pylon is incorporated in Kenya. Requests from foreign law enforcement agencies must be made through MLAT channels or via the Kenya DCI. We do not honour informal requests from foreign agencies directly.

KDPA compliance: All customer data is stored within Kenya. We operate under the Kenya Data Protection Act 2019. Data subject access requests can be submitted to privacy@pylon.digital.

CERT coordination: Pylon coordinates with AfricaCERT and KE-CIRT/CC on significant network security events. CERTs and CSIRTs may contact security@pylon.digital for coordinated disclosure or cross-network incidents.
