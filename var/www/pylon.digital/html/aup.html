Section 01

Introduction

This Acceptable Use Policy ("AUP") governs what you may and may not do on Pylon Digital's infrastructure and services. It applies to every customer, reseller, end user, and visitor — to anyone who uses any Pylon service or whose traffic passes through our network.

This AUP is incorporated by reference into Pylon's Terms of Service. By using any Pylon service, you agree to this AUP.

Why we have an AUP: We operate shared infrastructure. What one customer does affects others — spam from one VM gets our IP addresses blacklisted, affecting everyone's email deliverability. A DDoS launched from our network attracts legal attention for everyone. This AUP protects all customers, not just Pylon.

How we enforce it: We investigate reports before acting. For most violations, we contact the customer first and give a remediation window. For critical violations — active attacks, CSAM, phishing in progress — we act first and notify after. We explain our reasoning for every action we take.

Borderline cases: Not everything is black and white. If you're unsure whether your intended use is permitted, email hello@pylon.digital before you start — we'd rather say "yes, that's fine" in advance than suspend you after the fact.

Section 02

Permitted Uses

✓ Allowed

The following uses are clearly permitted on Pylon infrastructure. This list is illustrative, not exhaustive — if your use case is broadly similar, it's almost certainly fine.

Web & Application Hosting ✓ Allowed

Hosting websites, web applications, REST APIs, GraphQL APIs, SaaS platforms, e-commerce stores, portals, and similar internet-facing services. Commercial, personal, NGO, and government use all permitted.

Includes: WordPress, Joomla, Drupal, Laravel, Django, Node.js, Ruby on Rails, Next.js, static sites, and any other web application framework.

// Note: High-traffic sites with sustained very high egress should contact us in advance so we can ensure adequate capacity. This is for operational coordination, not restriction.
Email Servers ✓ Allowed

Running your own mail server (Postfix, Dovecot, Exim, or similar) for sending and receiving email for your domain. Transactional email (order confirmations, password resets, notifications) and opt-in newsletter sending are permitted.

We require that you honour unsubscribe requests within 48 hours and maintain your SPF, DKIM, and DMARC records. We provide free PTR record configuration for mail servers.

// Distinction: Transactional and opted-in email = permitted. Unsolicited bulk email (spam) = prohibited. See Section 4.
VPN Servers (Personal & Corporate) ✓ Allowed

Running WireGuard or OpenVPN servers for your own use or your organisation's remote team. Securing branch office connectivity via site-to-site tunnels. Protecting field staff connecting over untrusted networks.

Permitted provided: (a) the VPN serves identified users of your organisation or household, and (b) the underlying traffic through the VPN complies with this AUP.

// Distinction: Corporate / personal VPN = permitted. Open proxy or anonymous VPN service for unknown third parties = ask us first (see Section 3).
Containers, Kubernetes & Docker ✓ Allowed

Running containerised workloads — Docker, Docker Compose, Podman, K3s, RKE2, or any other container runtime. Full kernel access on Pylon VMs means no artificial container restrictions.

Multi-node Kubernetes clusters spanning multiple Pylon VMs are permitted. We can help with private network configuration between VMs for cluster use.

Databases & Storage ✓ Allowed

Self-managed or third-party database software — PostgreSQL, MySQL, MariaDB, MongoDB, Redis, Elasticsearch, ClickHouse, and others. Storing customer data, application data, logs, and backups for your own services.

Game Servers ✓ Allowed

Hosting game servers for online multiplayer games — Minecraft, Valheim, CS:GO, FiveM, and similar. Fair-use bandwidth applies — servers expected to use very high sustained bandwidth should notify us in advance.

Development & CI/CD ✓ Allowed

Development environments, staging servers, test environments, CI/CD runners (GitHub Actions, GitLab CI, Jenkins), build agents, code repositories, and documentation sites. Personal and team development infrastructure is a common and welcome use case.

DNS Servers & Resolvers ✓ Allowed

Running authoritative DNS servers for your own domains, or DNS resolvers for your organisation's internal network. DNSSEC signing servers and DNS-based service discovery for your infrastructure.

// Note: Open resolvers accessible to the general public can be misused for DNS amplification attacks. If running a resolver, please restrict it to your own IP ranges or authenticated clients.
Backup & Archival Storage ✓ Allowed

Using Pylon VMs or storage for backup targets, offsite archival, and disaster recovery replication. Running Restic, BorgBackup, rsync, Rclone, or similar tools to store backups from your other infrastructure.

Section 03

Conditional Uses

~ Ask Us First

The following uses may be permitted but require prior written approval from Pylon. Email hello@pylon.digital before proceeding. We'll usually respond with a "yes, here's how" or "yes, with these constraints" rather than a flat refusal — we'd rather know about it.

Cryptocurrency Mining ~ Ask us

CPU or GPU cryptocurrency mining is not permitted on VM plans without prior written approval. High CPU utilisation from mining degrades performance for other VMs on the same host — our VMs have dedicated vCPU but share the thermal and power envelope of the host.

Colocation customers may mine on their own hardware in our facility without restriction, since they have dedicated hardware with their own power allocation.

If you want to run mining on a Pylon VM, contact us — in some cases we can offer a dedicated host arrangement that removes the shared-resource concern.

// Why we ask: It's a resource intensity issue for VMs, not a moral judgement about cryptocurrency.
High-Bandwidth Streaming & CDN Origins ~ Ask us

Video streaming platforms, CDN origin servers, or any service expected to sustain very high outbound bandwidth (consistently above 500 Mbps) should contact us before launch. We can accommodate high-bandwidth use cases but need to plan capacity.

We operate on fair-use bandwidth — no hard cap — but sustained multi-gigabit output from a single VM impacts other customers on the uplink. We'll work with you on a suitable arrangement, which may include a dedicated uplink or custom pricing.

Security Research & Penetration Testing ~ Ask us

Legitimate security research, penetration testing, and vulnerability scanning against your own systems is generally fine. Scanning or testing infrastructure that you own or have written authorisation to test is permitted.

If your research involves outbound scanning of third-party IPs or generating traffic patterns that resemble attacks (even against consenting targets), notify us in advance. We receive abuse reports from other networks and need to be able to explain why specific traffic is legitimate.

// Clear line: Scanning your own infrastructure = fine. Scanning the internet = ask us. Scanning someone else's infrastructure without permission = prohibited (Section 4).
Tor Exit Nodes ~ Ask us

Running a Tor relay or bridge is permitted without approval. Running a Tor exit node requires prior written approval. Exit nodes generate significant abuse reports because third parties see our IP address as the source of potentially harmful traffic.

We evaluate exit node requests case-by-case. If approved, we'll work with you on exit policy configuration to reduce abuse report volume.

Open Proxy or Public VPN Service ~ Ask us

Operating a VPN or proxy service open to anonymous third-party users (not your own organisation) requires prior approval. The concern is accountability — when unknown users pass traffic through our IP space, we cannot ensure AUP compliance from those users.

If you're building a commercial privacy VPN product, contact us to discuss a reseller arrangement that includes appropriate AUP accountability.

Very Large File Distribution ~ Ask us

Distributing very large files (software releases, OS images, media) at scale from a Pylon VM may affect other customers on shared uplink. If you expect peak download demand exceeding a few hundred Mbps, talk to us first — we can often accommodate this or recommend pairing with a CDN for delivery.

Section 04

Prohibited Uses

✗ Prohibited

The following uses are prohibited on Pylon infrastructure without exception. Violations result in immediate suspension, account termination, and where applicable, referral to law enforcement. These are not negotiable — email us if you think we've made an error, but don't proceed in the meantime.

Spam & Unsolicited Bulk Email ✗ Prohibited

Sending unsolicited email in bulk — whether commercial, political, or any other purpose — is prohibited. This includes:

  • —Sending email to addresses harvested without opt-in consent
  • —Operating open mail relays that forward email for third parties
  • —Sending email after unsubscribe requests are received
  • —Renting or purchasing email lists and mailing to them from our infrastructure
// Why: Spam from our IP range affects every customer who sends legitimate email — our IPs get blacklisted and email deliverability suffers for all of us.
DDoS, Network Attacks & Disruption ✗ Prohibited

Launching denial-of-service attacks, distributed denial-of-service attacks, or any coordinated disruption of network availability against any target — including other Pylon customers. This includes:

  • —Volumetric attacks (UDP flood, ICMP flood, SYN flood)
  • —Amplification attacks (DNS amplification, NTP amplification)
  • —Application-layer attacks (HTTP flood, Slowloris)
  • —Participating in a botnet launching attacks
// Why: Attacks from our IP space result in our addresses being blocked network-wide, affecting all customers. We also have legal obligations to protect our upstream and KIXP peers.
Phishing, Fraud & Impersonation ✗ Prohibited

Hosting websites, applications, or services designed to deceive users into providing credentials, financial information, or personal data under false pretences. This includes:

  • —Fake login pages impersonating banks, mobile money services, or other platforms
  • —Fraudulent investment or cryptocurrency platforms
  • —Fake government or official websites
  • —Advance fee fraud (419) or romance scam infrastructure
Malware, Botnet & C2 Infrastructure ✗ Prohibited

Hosting, distributing, commanding, or controlling malicious software of any kind:

  • —Ransomware command-and-control servers
  • —Botnet C2 infrastructure
  • —Malware distribution sites or dropper infrastructure
  • —Remote access trojans (RATs) or keylogger delivery infrastructure
  • —Exploit kits or drive-by download infrastructure
Unauthorised Port Scanning & Brute Force ✗ Prohibited

Scanning, probing, or brute-forcing systems that you do not own or have explicit written authorisation to test. This includes:

  • —Internet-wide port scans (Masscan, Zmap, Shodan-style scanning)
  • —SSH, RDP, or service brute-force attacks against third-party systems
  • —Credential stuffing attacks against web applications
  • —Vulnerability exploitation against systems you don't own
Illegal Content Under Kenyan Law ✗ Prohibited

Hosting, storing, or distributing any content that is illegal under the laws of Kenya, including but not limited to:

  • —Content that violates the Computer Misuse and Cybercrimes Act 2018
  • —Content that incites violence, hatred, or discrimination on protected grounds
  • —Content that violates intellectual property law without authorisation
  • —Content that defames individuals in violation of Kenyan law
IP Spoofing & Network Forgery ✗ Prohibited

Sending network traffic with forged source IP addresses (IP spoofing). We enforce BCP38 ingress filtering at our network edge — traffic with spoofed source addresses is dropped automatically. Attempting to circumvent this is prohibited.

Also prohibited: forging email headers, BGP route injection, ARP spoofing within shared network segments, and any other form of network-level impersonation.

Interference with Other Customers ✗ Prohibited

Any activity that deliberately or recklessly degrades the service quality experienced by other Pylon customers. This includes sustained CPU abuse on oversold VMs (though we don't oversell), network flooding that congests shared uplinks, and intentionally generating abuse reports that result in IP blacklisting affecting others.

Section 05

Zero Tolerance Activities

Immediate termination

The following violations result in immediate account termination without notice, without refund, and with mandatory referral to Kenyan law enforcement and relevant international agencies. There is no remediation window for these.

Child Sexual Abuse Material (CSAM): Hosting, storing, distributing, or facilitating access to child sexual abuse material of any kind. We report all instances to the Kenya National Police Service, the Internet Watch Foundation (IWF), and the National Center for Missing & Exploited Children (NCMEC). We preserve all account data for law enforcement.
Terrorism & violent extremism: Hosting content that recruits for, finances, incites, or facilitates acts of terrorism or mass violence, as defined under Kenyan law and relevant international frameworks.
Active attacks on critical infrastructure: Launching attacks on power grids, water systems, financial systems, healthcare infrastructure, or government systems — in Kenya or any other country. These are treated as the most serious possible AUP violation.

For CSAM reports specifically: we do not give the account holder any notice before acting. We act immediately, preserve data, and report to authorities. No amount of good-faith explanation changes the response. If you discover CSAM on your server because it was placed there by an attacker, contact us immediately — we will work with you cooperatively in that scenario.

Section 06

Consequences of Violations

Our response to AUP violations is proportionate to the severity. Here is what to expect at each tier:

Severity Examples Our response
Critical Active DDoS launch, CSAM, active phishing campaign, botnet C2, malware C2 Immediate null-route or suspension — no advance notice. Law enforcement referral where required. No refund. Customer contacted after action.Response: within 1 hour of detection or report
High Spam campaigns, unauthorised port scanning, open relay, brute-force attacks, IP spoofing Customer contacted immediately. 6-hour remediation window. Suspension if not resolved. Possible permanent termination for repeat violations.Response: within 4 hours
Medium Copyright infringement, unsolicited email to purchased lists, high bandwidth without notice Customer contacted. 24–48 hour remediation window. Traffic may be throttled during investigation. Warning recorded on account.Response: within 24 hours

Appeals: If you believe a suspension or termination was made in error, email hello@pylon.digital with your reference number and explanation. We review all appeals within 2 business days. If we made a mistake, we'll restore your service immediately and apologise.

Compromised accounts: If your server was compromised and used for prohibited activities without your knowledge, tell us immediately. We distinguish between customers who are victims of compromise and those who deliberately violate the AUP — your response and cooperation matter in that assessment.

Section 07

Resellers & Downstream Customers

If you resell Pylon services to your own customers, you are responsible for ensuring that those customers comply with this AUP. Your customers' violations are your violations.

  • You must include AUP provisions at least as restrictive as ours in your own customer agreements. If you'd like a template or reference to our AUP, you may link to this page.
  • When Pylon contacts you about a downstream customer violation, you are responsible for notifying and managing that customer. We expect a response within the same timeframe as a direct customer.
  • Persistent violations by your downstream customers — especially if you appear to be facilitating or ignoring them — may result in termination of your reseller account.
  • You may not resell services to customers on OFAC sanctions lists, UN sanctions lists, or Kenyan government sanctions lists.
  • If you receive an abuse report relating to one of your customers, forward it to us with context. We investigate abuse reports whether they come from the reseller or directly.
Section 08

Reporting AUP Violations

If you observe activity on our network that violates this AUP, please report it. Every report is read by a human and investigated. Here's how to reach us:

Urgent / active incidents: Email abuse@pylon.digital directly — monitored 24/7. Include the source IP, timestamps in EAT or UTC, and any log samples. Do not use the form below for active attacks or CSAM — email directly.

For non-urgent reports, use the form below. The more detail you provide, the faster we can investigate and act.

Reports are read by a human NOC operator. We do not share your identity with the reported party. False or malicious reports may be referred to Kenyan authorities. We will confirm receipt and follow up on the outcome.
Section 09

Changes to This AUP

We may update this AUP to reflect changes in our services, legal requirements, or observed abuse patterns. We'll notify you before material changes take effect.

Material changes (adding prohibited uses, tightening enforcement) will be communicated via email to your registered address at least 14 days before taking effect. You may terminate your service without penalty during this period if you cannot comply with the updated AUP.

Clarifications and expansions of permitted uses may be made without advance notice — we'll always tell you when we're giving you more latitude, not less.

Questions about this AUP or specific use cases can be sent to hello@pylon.digital. Abuse reports go to abuse@pylon.digital.

Our enforcement philosophy: We would rather help a confused customer understand the rules than terminate their account. If you're unsure about something, ask before you do it. We respond honestly and quickly — usually "yes, that's fine" or "yes, with this constraint." We are not looking for reasons to suspend accounts. We are looking to keep our network clean and fair for everyone.
