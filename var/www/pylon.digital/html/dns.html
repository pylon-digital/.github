// Record Types

Every record type you need.
No limitations.

We support all standard DNS record types. Wildcard records supported across all types. No artificial restrictions on record count per zone.

A
Address (IPv4)
Maps a hostname to an IPv4 address. The most common record — used for web servers, mail servers, and any internet-facing service.
example.co.ke → 196.201.x.x
AAAA
Address (IPv6)
Maps a hostname to an IPv6 address. Essential for dual-stack deployments. All Pylon services support IPv6 natively.
example.co.ke → 2c0f:xxxx::1
CNAME
Canonical Name
Alias for another hostname. Commonly used for www. pointing to the apex domain, or CDN configurations.
www → yoursite.co.ke
MX
Mail Exchange
Specifies which mail servers handle email for your domain. Priority values determine which server is tried first.
@ → mail.pylon.digital (pri 10)
TXT
Text
Stores arbitrary text. Used for SPF, DKIM, DMARC, domain verification (Google, Microsoft, etc.), and ACME challenges.
@ → "v=spf1 include:... ~all"
SRV
Service
Specifies host and port for specific services. Used by VoIP (SIP), XMPP, Microsoft services, and game servers.
_sip._tcp → sip.example.co.ke:5060
CAA
Certification Authority
Restricts which certificate authorities can issue SSL certificates for your domain. Recommended security practice alongside DNSSEC.
@ → 0 issue "letsencrypt.org"
PTR
Pointer (Reverse DNS)
Maps an IP address back to a hostname. Critical for mail server reputation. We manage PTR records for all Pylon-assigned IPs at no extra charge.
196.201.x.x → mail.yourco.co.ke
Also supported: NS (delegation), SOA (zone authority), DS (DNSSEC delegation), NAPTR (telecom / SIP), SSHFP (SSH key fingerprint), TLSA (certificate pinning). Wildcard records (*.domain.co.ke) supported on all types.

// DNSSEC

DNSSEC explained —
and why it matters.

DNS without DNSSEC is vulnerable to cache poisoning — where an attacker injects false records to redirect your users to malicious sites. DNSSEC prevents this with cryptographic validation.

// Chain of trust — how validation works

1
Root zone — ICANN
The DNS root zone is signed by ICANN's Root Signing Key. All DNSSEC validation chains start here — it's the anchor of trust for the entire system.
2
TLD zone — .ke / .com / .org
The .ke TLD (managed by KENIC) is DNSSEC-signed. Your DS record is submitted here — it tells the TLD that your zone is signed and provides the key fingerprint.
3
Your zone — signed by Pylon
We sign all records in your zone with your DNSSEC key pair. We generate the key pair, handle the DS record submission to your registrar, and rotate keys automatically.
4
Resolver validates the chain
When a DNSSEC-aware resolver (like Google 8.8.8.8 or Cloudflare 1.1.1.1) looks up your domain, it validates the full chain from root to your zone. If any signature is invalid, the query fails — protecting your users.
Algorithm we use
ECDSAP256SHA256 (Algorithm 13) — the modern standard. Smaller keys than RSA, faster validation, and recommended by IETF for new deployments.
What we manage for you
Key pair generation · DS record submission to registrar · RRSIG signing of all zone records · Automatic key rollover (ZSK every 90 days, KSK annually) · NSEC3 for zone enumeration protection.
What you need to do
Nothing ongoing. On initial setup, you add the NS records we give you at your registrar — we handle DNSSEC from there. If your registrar supports DS record submission via API, we can automate that too.
Check your domain's DNSSEC status
Run: dig +dnssec yourcompany.co.ke A
Or check: dnssec-debugger.verisignlabs.com
Look for RRSIG records in the answer and ad flag in the header.
Why .co.ke domains especially benefit
KENIC supports DNSSEC on .co.ke domains, but most Kenyan hosting providers don't configure it. DNSSEC protection is especially valuable for fintech, healthcare, and NGO domains where users need assurance they're on the real site.

// FAQ

DNS questions.

Can't find your answer? Email us or check the full FAQ.

Do I have to use Pylon for hosting to use Pylon DNS?+

No. Our standalone DNS plans are for any domain, regardless of where your server or hosting is. You point your domain's nameservers to us at your registrar, and we handle DNS from there. Your website, email, and other services can be hosted anywhere.

If you're a Pylon customer (VM, hosting, email, VPN, or colocation), DNS hosting is included free for all your domains — you don't need a separate DNS plan.

How do I switch my domain's nameservers to Pylon?+

Log into your domain registrar (e.g. KENIC for .co.ke, GoDaddy, Namecheap, Africa Online) and update the nameserver records to:

ns1.pylon.digital and ns2.pylon.digital

Before you change, import your existing zone records to Pylon DNS so there's no gap. DNS propagation typically takes 15–30 minutes for .co.ke domains and up to 24 hours for some international domains. We'll confirm when your domain is resolving correctly through our nameservers.

What is DNSSEC and do I need it?+

DNSSEC (Domain Name System Security Extensions) adds cryptographic signatures to your DNS records. It prevents attackers from poisoning DNS caches with fake records that redirect your users to malicious sites.

We recommend DNSSEC for any domain where users need to trust they're on the real site — which is most business domains, especially in fintech, healthcare, and NGO sectors. We enable and manage it automatically — you don't need to understand the cryptography, just tell us your registrar and we'll handle the DS record submission.

How quickly do DNS changes propagate?+

Changes to records on our authoritative nameservers take effect immediately. How quickly they propagate globally depends on the TTL of the record being changed — if a record has a 300-second (5-minute) TTL, cached copies in resolvers around the world expire within 5 minutes.

If you need fast propagation (for a migration or failover), set your TTL to 60 seconds at least 24 hours before the change — so existing caches expire quickly. After the change, you can raise the TTL back to a normal value.

Can I import my existing DNS zone from another provider?+

Yes. Export your zone as a BIND-format zone file from your current provider (most support this) and import it via our web panel or API. All records transfer instantly — no manual re-entry. We verify the import before you change your nameservers, so there's no risk of missing records.

What happens if I want to leave Pylon DNS?+

Export your zone as a BIND file any time via the panel or API — it's always available, no export lock or fee. Take that file to any other DNS provider, import it, and change your nameservers. We don't make leaving difficult. The export is standard BIND format that every DNS provider accepts.

Do you support .co.ke domains with DNSSEC?+

Yes. KENIC (the .ke registry) supports DNSSEC on .co.ke, .or.ke, .go.ke, and other .ke second-level domains. We generate your DS record and provide exact instructions for submitting it to your registrar or directly to KENIC. If your registrar doesn't support DS record submission, contact us — we'll help you navigate the process.

Is there a limit on DNS queries or records?+

No query limits on any plan — we don't meter or throttle DNS queries. High-traffic domains are welcome. Record count per zone is unlimited, and you can have as many zones as your plan allows.

If you're expecting unusually high query volumes (hundreds of millions per month) or have very specific performance requirements, talk to us and we'll make sure our infrastructure is sized appropriately for your use case.
