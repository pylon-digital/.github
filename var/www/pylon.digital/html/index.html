// FAQ

Common questions.

Can't find what you're looking for? Email us.

Where is your infrastructure located?

Our facility is in Nairobi's CBD, co-located within a carrier-neutral datacenter with generator backup, redundant cooling, and direct KIXP peering. Our upstream transit is provided by Liquid Telecom.

What operating systems can I run on a VM?

Any Linux distribution — Ubuntu, Debian, Fedora, Rocky, Alpine, Arch. Custom ISOs are supported. We don't currently offer Windows VMs but can accommodate it for colo customers.

How does billing work?

We bill monthly in KES or USD. Payment via M-Pesa (Till/Paybill), bank transfer (KCB / Equity), or card. Invoices are sent on the 1st of each month.

Is my data protected under Kenyan law?

Yes. All data is stored within Kenya and we operate in compliance with the Kenya Data Protection Act (KDPA) 2019. We do not transfer data outside Kenya without explicit consent.

Do you offer backups?

Daily snapshots are available as an add-on for KES 150/month per VM. Colocation customers are responsible for their own backup strategy, though we can discuss managed backup options.

What's included in the 99.9% SLA?

Network availability and power to your rack or VM host. SLA credits are applied automatically if we fall below 99.9% in a calendar month. We publish our status publicly at status.pylon.digital.

Can I get remote hands support for colo?

Yes — we offer remote hands during business hours (Mon–Sat, 8am–6pm EAT) at KES 2,500/hour. Emergency out-of-hours support is available at a higher rate.

Do you offer student or NGO pricing?

Students with a valid university email get KES 2,000 extra credit on signup. NGOs and nonprofits operating in East Africa can contact us for discounted rates.
